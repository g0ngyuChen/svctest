Abstract

Singing voice conversion (SVC) aims to transform the vocal timbre of a source singer into that of a target singer while preserving the original musical content. Although recent zero-shot SVC systems achieve strong performance on clean a cappella vocals, they still struggle under real-world production conditions, where residual harmonies, separation artifacts, and noisy F0 extraction frequently occur. To bridge this gap, we propose YingMusic-SVC, a real-world robust zero-shot SVC framework tailored for industrial pipelines. Our system combines an RVC-based timbre shifter, an F0-aware fine-grained timbre adaptor, and a frequency-aware energy-balanced rectified flow objective, together with a multi-stage training pipeline (CPT → SFT → Flow-GRPO RL). Extensive experiments on difficulty-graded test suites demonstrate that YingMusic-SVC consistently improves speaker similarity, intelligibility, and perceptual aesthetics over strong baselines such as Seed-VC, particularly in challenging scenarios with mixture vocals and separated lead tracks.

Method

Figure 2: Architecture of YingMusic-SVC, including timbre shifter, F0-aware timbre adaptor, and DiT-based rectified flow generator.

YingMusic-SVC builds upon a DiT-based rectified flow model operating on log-mel spectrograms. The input vocal is encoded by three frozen encoders to obtain content, global timbre, and F0 features. In parallel, an RVC-based timbre shifter converts the source singing into randomized timbres, producing a shift-content representation that explicitly suppresses source-speaker leakage. A lightweight F0-aware timbre adaptor then transforms the global target embedding into a fine-grained timbre map aligned with the local pitch contour. All conditioning features are temporally aligned to the mel frames and concatenated in a Seed-VC-style “leading/semantic” masking scheme, where early frames primarily provide timbre cues and later frames emphasize content. The rectified flow model is trained with a frequency-aware energy-balanced loss that assigns higher weights to low-energy high-frequency components and later denoising steps, encouraging sharper harmonic details in singing voice generation.

Evaluation Overview

We evaluate YingMusic-SVC on a difficulty-graded test suite derived from internal multi-track singing data. We randomly sample 100 clips of 10–20 s that contain semantically meaningful singing content. The GT Leading set uses only the clean lead vocal track, while the Mix Vocal set merges lead and backing tracks to simulate real outputs from source-separation front-ends. In addition, we report results on an Ours Leading set constructed from vocals separated by our in-house Band-Roformer model, reflecting the full industrial pipeline. For each setting, we select five target timbres covering both genders and different age ranges, and all songs and speakers are unseen during training.

For objective metrics, we report speaker embedding cosine similarity (SPK-SIM), character error rate (CER), log-F0 Pearson correlation (LogF0PCC), and aesthetic scores (CE/CU) from Audiobox Aesthetics. For subjective evaluation, we conduct CMOS and SMOS listening tests with 10 listeners on 20 randomly selected source–target pairs, comparing YingMusic-SVC to strong baselines including FreeSVC and Seed-VC.
